**Sound Card** – A sound card is a hardware component that handles **audio processing** for a computer, allowing it to **output sound to speakers or headphones and input sound from microphones**.

A sound card is the part of a computer that processes and manages audio input and output.

- Converts digital audio signals into analog sound (so you can hear it)
- Converts analog sound (microphone input) into digital data
- Processes audio effects and sound quality
- Enables speakers, headphones, and microphones to work

### Types of sound cards

- **Integrated Sound Card** → built into the **[[Motherboard]]** (most common)
- **Dedicated Sound Card** → installed via **[[Expansion Slots (PCIe)]]** for higher audio quality
- **External Sound Card** → connected via USB for portable audio processing
#### How it works?

Computer Data → Sound Card → Speakers/Headphones (Sound Output)
Microphone → Sound Card → Computer Data (Sound Input)

#### Why it is Important? 

- Enables audio playback and recording
- Improves sound quality in music, games, and video
- Supports communication apps (calls, meetings, voice chat)
- Reduces noise and improves audio clarity in advanced cards

In short, a sound card is a hardware component that processes audio signals, enabling a computer to play and record sound.
