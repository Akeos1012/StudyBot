**Disaster Recovery** – The **process of restoring IT systems, data, and operations after a disruptive event such as hardware failure, cyberattack, natural disaster, or system crash**.

#### What it Involves?

- Restoring [[Data]] from backups
- Recovering [[Server]] and System operations
- Restarting critical applications
- Minimizing downtime
- Ensuring business continuity

#### What it Protect Against?

- [[Hardware]] failure
- [[Cybersecurity]] attacks (e.g., ransomware)
- Power Outage
- Natural Disaster (floods, earthquakes)
- [[Software]] crashes or corruption

#### Simple analogy

Disaster recovery is like:

having a spare house, spare keys, and copies of everything ready in case your main house burns down.

In short, Disaster Recovery is the process of restoring IT systems, data, and operations after a major disruption to ensure continuity and minimize downtime.
