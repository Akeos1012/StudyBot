**Amplifier** – An amplifier is a **device or electronic component that increases the strength of a weak electrical signal by boosting its amplitude using external power, without changing the original information**.

- Increases the amplitude of [[Signal]] (voltage, current, or power)
- Uses external power to boost weak inputs
- Built using components like [[Transistor]] or [[Operational Amplifier]]
- Common in [[Audio Processing]] and communication systems
- Works within [[Analog Circuit]] systems
- Preserves the original shape and information of the signal

In short, an amplifier is an electronic device that increases the strength of a weak electrical signal using external power while preserving the original information.
