**Audio Drivers** – Audio drivers are the **software components that allow the operating system to communicate with and control audio hardware such as speakers, microphones, and sound cards so that sound can be properly recorded, processed, and played back**.

They act as the bridge between audio hardware and the system, ensuring sound data is correctly interpreted and delivered.

- Translate [[Operating System]] audio instructions into hardware-level signals
- Enable communication between [[Sound Card]], speakers, and microphones
- Manage audio input (recording) and output (playback)
- Support [[Audio Processing]] like volume control, effects, and mixing
- Ensure compatibility between different [[Hardware]] audio devices and software
- Used in [[Multimedia Systems]] and [[Device Drivers]] layer of the system

Audio drivers are system software components that control how sound is handled between the operating system and audio hardware.

**Example:**
- Realtek HD Audio Driver enabling sound on laptops
- NVIDIA/AMD audio drivers for HDMI sound output
- USB headset drivers for voice calls and gaming audio
- Bluetooth audio drivers for wireless headphones

#### How System Works?

Application → [[Operating System]] → Audio Driver → [[Sound Card]] → Speakers / Headphones → Output Sound

In short, an operating system component that manages communication between software and audio hardware to ensure proper sound input and output.
