**Amplifier Circuit** – An amplifier circuit is a **type of electronic circuit that increases the strength (amplitude) of a weak electrical signal without changing its original form or information**.

- Boosts weak signals into stronger outputs
- Uses components like [[Transistor]], [[Operational Amplifier]], [[Resistor]], and [[Capacitor]]
- Works with [[Analog Circuit]] signals (continuous signals)
- Common in [[Audio Processing]] and communication systems
- Does not create new data, only increases signal strength
- Used in both low-power and high-power electronic systems

An amplifier circuit is an electronic system that increases signal strength while preserving the original signal shape and information.

___
### Types of Amplifier Circuits

- **Voltage Amplifier** → increases voltage level of a signal
- **Current Amplifier** → increases current flow
- **Power Amplifier** → increases overall power for output devices
- **Audio Amplifier** → used in speakers and sound systems
- **Operational Amplifier (Op-Amp) Circuits** → used in precision signal processing

**Examples:**
- Smartphone speaker amplifier
- Microphone signal booster
- Radio transmission systems
- Guitar amplifier
- Hearing aid devices
