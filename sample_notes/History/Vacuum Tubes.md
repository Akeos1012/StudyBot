**Vacuum tubes** - An early electronic components that **control the flow of electric current inside a sealed glass tube using a vacuum**.

They were widely used in early computers like **[[ENIAC]]** before modern transistors existed. Vacuum tubes are old electronic switches that control electricity in early computers.

- Act as electronic switches (ON/OFF states)
- Amplify electrical signals
- Process binary data in early computing systems
- Enable basic logic operations in early machines
#### Limitation

- Very large and bulky
- Consumes a lot of electricity
- Generates a lot of heat
- Breaks down easily
- Slow compared to modern components

### What replaced them

**Vacuum tubes were replaced by:**
- [[Transistor]] → smaller, faster, more efficient
- [[Logic Gates]] → built from transistors for digital computing

In short, Vacuum Tubes are early electronic switches used in first-generation computers to control and amplify electrical signals before transistors replaced them.
