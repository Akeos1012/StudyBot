### First Completed Electronic General-Purpose Computer

**ENIAC** -  A Electronic Numerical Integrator and [[Computer]] was the first programmable electronic general-purpose digital computer. It was developed at the University of Pennsylvania between 1943 and 1945 for the U.S. Army to compute complex artillery firing tables during World War II.

Unlike the mechanical Analytical Engine, ENIAC used electronic components and could perform calculations thousands of times faster, showing how Babbage’s ideas evolved into working computers.

- It used [[Vacuum Tubes]] instead of mechanical parts for computation
- It could be reprogrammed to solve different types of problems
- It significantly influenced the design of modern electronic computers

ENIAC’s success demonstrated that electronic computers were practical, which led to the development of more advanced and commercially usable systems such as the **[[UNIVAC I]]**, the first widely produced commercial computer in the United States.
