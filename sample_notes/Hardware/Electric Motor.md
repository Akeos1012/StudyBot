**Electric Motor** – An electric motor is a **device that converts electrical energy into mechanical energy using electromagnetic forces to produce rotational motion**.

- Uses [[Electric Current]] to create motion
- Works through interaction between Magnetic Field and electric charge
- Converts electrical energy into mechanical rotation
- Found in many Electronic Devices and machines
- Depends on components like coils, magnets, and rotors
- Controlled by [[Circuit]] design and power input

An electric motor is an electromechanical device that produces motion by using electricity and magnetism.

___
## Types of Electric Motor

- **AC Motor** → runs on alternating current ([[AC]])
- **DC Motor** → runs on direct current ([[DC]])
- **Brushless Motor** → more efficient, used in drones and electronics
- **Stepper Motor** → moves in precise steps (used in printers, robotics)
- **Servo Motor** → controlled for exact position and movement

**Examples:**
- Electric fans
- Washing machines
- Hard disk drives
- Electric cars
- Computer cooling fans
- Robotics systems
##### How it Works?

Electric Current → Magnetic Field Generated → Force on Coil → Rotation → Mechanical Motion

In short, An electric motor is a device that converts electrical energy into mechanical motion using electromagnetic forces, enabling machines and devices to perform physical movement.
