**Capacitor** – A capacitor is a **passive electronic component that stores electrical energy temporarily in an electric field between two conductive plates separated by an insulating material (dielectric)**.

In simple terms:

It’s a tiny energy “storage tank” that charges and releases electricity when needed.

- Stores electrical energy in an electric field
- Made of two conductive plates separated by a dielectric
- Charges when connected to a [[PSU]] or circuit
- Releases energy when the circuit needs it
- Used for filtering, timing, and stabilizing voltage in [[Electronic Devices]]
- Works alongside components like [[Resistor]] and [[Inductor]] in circuits

A capacitor is an electronic component that temporarily stores and releases electrical energy to support stable and controlled circuit operation.

---
### Types of Capacitor

- **Ceramic Capacitor** → small, used in general electronics
- **Electrolytic Capacitor** → large capacitance, used for power filtering
- **Tantalum Capacitor** → stable and reliable for precision circuits
- **Film Capacitor** → used in audio and high-frequency systems
- **Variable Capacitor** → capacitance can be adjusted manually

**Examples:**
- Smartphone power regulation circuits
- Computer motherboard voltage smoothing
- Camera flash systems storing quick energy bursts
- Audio systems filtering noise
- Power supply smoothing in electronics
