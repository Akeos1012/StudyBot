**[[PSU]]** – (Power Supply Unit) The component of a computer that **converts electricity from an external power source into usable power for internal hardware components**.

It distributes the correct voltage and current to parts like the **[[CPU]]**, **[[GPU]]**, **[[Motherboard]]**, and storage devices.

- Converts AC power (from wall outlet) to DC power (used by computer parts)
- Supplies stable voltage to hardware components
- Protects the system from power fluctuations
- Distributes power through cables to all internal devices

#### How it works?

Wall Outlet (AC Power)  
↓  
PSU (converts & regulates power)  
↓  
Motherboard, CPU, GPU, Storage, etc.

#### Why is it Importance?

- Without a PSU, no computer component can function
- Ensures system stability and safety
- Prevents hardware damage from unstable electricity

In short, A PSU is the component that supplies, converts, and regulates electrical power to all parts of a computer system. PSU is the **heart of the computer’s power system.**
