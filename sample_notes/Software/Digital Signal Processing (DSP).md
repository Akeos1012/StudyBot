**Digital Signal Processing** – (DSP) is the **use of mathematical algorithms and computer systems to analyze, modify, and improve digital signals such as audio, images, and sensor data**.

- Converts real-world signals into Digital Signal form
- Filters noise from audio or images
- Compresses data (like MP3, video codecs)
- Enhances sound quality in [[Audio Processing]]
- Analyzes signals for patterns (speech, radar, etc.)
- Filtering (noise removal)
- Compression (MP3, JPEG)
- Fourier Transform (frequency analysis)
- Signal smoothing
- Pattern detection
#### Where DSP is used?

- [[Audio Processing]] (music, voice calls)
- [[Image Processing]] (filters, enhancement)
- [[Video Processing]]
- [[Telecommunications]]
- Radar and Medical Imaging systems

#### How it works?

1. Take an analog signal (sound/light/etc.)
2. Convert it into digital form using [[Sampling]]
3. Apply mathematical operations (filters, transforms)
4. Output improved or analyzed signal

In short, Digital Signal Processing (DSP) is the use of algorithms and computation to analyze, modify, and improve digital signals such as audio, images, and sensor data.
