**Edge Devices** – Edge devices are **physical computing devices that collect, process, and sometimes analyze data close to where it is generated, instead of sending everything to a central cloud or server**.

- Collect and generate [[Data]] from the real world
- Perform local processing before sending data to [[Cloud Computing]]
- Reduce [[Latency]] by processing information closer to the user
- Common in [[Edge Computing]] environments
- Often connected to [[IoT]] systems
- Can work independently or together with central servers

**Example:**
- Smart cameras analyzing video locally
- Smartwatches tracking health data
- Self-driving car sensors processing road conditions
- Smart thermostats adjusting temperature automatically
- Industrial sensors in smart factories

#### How System Works?

Data Source → Edge Device (Local Processing) → Quick Decision → Optional Cloud Sync → Storage/Analysis

In short, Edge Devices are physical computing devices that process and analyze data close to its source to reduce latency, improve efficiency, and minimize reliance on centralized cloud systems.
