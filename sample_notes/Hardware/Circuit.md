**Circuit** – A circuit is a **closed path or loop that allows electric current to flow and power electrical or electronic components using a source of electrical energy**.

- Provides a closed loop for [[Electric Current]] to flow
- Contains components like [[Resistor]], [[Capacitor]], and [[Switch]]
- Requires a power source like a Battery or power supply
- Can be simple (one loop) or complex (multiple branches)
- Found in all [[Electronic Devices]] and systems
- Controlled by principles of [[Voltage]] and [[Resistance]]

A circuit is a complete electrical pathway that enables the flow and control of electricity to perform useful work.

____
### Types of Circuit

- **Series Circuit** → components connected in a single path
- **Parallel Circuit** → multiple paths for current flow
- **Open Circuit** → broken path, no current flows
- **Closed Circuit** → complete path, current flows
- **Short Circuit** → unintended low-resistance path causing high current

**Examples:**
- Phone charging circuit
- Laptop motherboard circuits
- Household electrical wiring
- LED lighting systems
- Computer power supply circuits

#### How it works

Power Source → Conducting Path → Electrical Components → Controlled Current Flow → Output (light, motion, heat, etc.)

##### Simple analogy

A circuit is like:

a complete water pipeline—if the pipe is broken, water stops flowing; if it’s closed, water moves continuously.

In short, a circuit is a closed loop that allows electric current to flow through components and enables electrical energy to be used in devices and systems.
