**Bernoulli numbers** - are a special sequence of numbers that appear in higher-level mathematics, especially in **calculus, number theory, and algorithm analysis**.

**They look like this:**
1, −1/2, 1/6, 0, −1/30, 0, 1/42, ...

*Most of them alternate and many are zero after a certain pattern.*

### Why they are useful in Coding?

they appear in real computer science and programming tasks:

### 1. Algorithm optimization

**Used in formulas that improve performance of:**
- summation algorithms
- numerical computations
- mathematical modeling

### 2. Big computations (scientific computing)

**Used in:**
- physics simulations
- engineering software
- financial modeling

### 3. Series expansions in computing

**Appear in:**
- Taylor series
- numerical approximation methods
- symbolic math engines (like Mathematica, [[Python]] [[Libraries]])

### 4. Performance shortcuts

Instead of looping through large sums, Bernoulli-based formulas can reduce computation time from:

O(n) loops → to mathematical formula form

In short, Bernoulli numbers are special mathematical constants used to simplify complex summations and formulas, making certain types of computation faster and more efficient in advanced algorithms and scientific programming.
