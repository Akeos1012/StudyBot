**Internal Drive Bays** – Internal drive bays are physical mounting spaces inside a computer case where storage devices like **[[Storage Devices]] (HDDs and SSDs)** are installed.

They provide structure, power connection, and data connection points for internal storage. Internal drive bays are slots inside a computer case used to install storage drives like HDDs and SSDs.

- Hold storage devices securely inside the case
- Allow connection to the **[[Motherboard]]** and **[[Power Supply Unit]]**
- Organize internal storage for better airflow and cable management
- Support upgrading or adding more storage

### Types of internal drive bays

- **3.5-inch bays** → mainly for HDDs
- **2.5-inch bays** → mainly for SSDs
- **M.2 slots (on motherboard)** → small SSDs mounted directly on board
- **Hot-swappable bays (advanced systems)** → allow drives to be removed without shutting down

#### How it works? 

Storage Drive → Drive Bay → Motherboard + PSU → System Storage Access

The drive bay physically holds the drive while the system connects to it for data access.

#### Why is it Important? 

- Keeps storage components organized
- Improves cooling and airflow inside the case
- Makes upgrades and replacements easier
- Supports multiple storage devices in one system

In short, Internal drive bays are mounting spaces inside a computer case used to securely install and connect storage devices to the system.
