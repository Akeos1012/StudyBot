**Cooling System** – The cooling system is a set of hardware components that **removes heat from computer parts to prevent overheating and maintain stable performance**, especially in high-power components like the **[[CPU]]** and **[[GPU]]**.

**Computer parts generate heat when they process data. If they get too hot:**

- Performance slows down (thermal throttling)
- Hardware can become unstable
- Components may get damaged over time

___

### Main types of cooling systems

#### 1. Air Cooling

- Uses fans to move heat away
- Includes heatsinks (metal structures that absorb heat)
- Most common in standard PCs and laptops
#### 2. Liquid Cooling

- Uses liquid (usually water-based coolant) to transfer heat
- More efficient than air cooling
- Common in gaming PCs and high-performance systems
#### 3. Passive Cooling

- No fans or moving parts
- Uses only heatsinks
- Found in low-power devices like tablets or embedded systems

___

#### How it works?

CPU/GPU → Heat → Heatsink/Fan or Liquid System → Heat released outside system

In short, a cooling system is hardware that removes excess heat from computer components to maintain performance and prevent overheating.
