**Analog Circuit** – An analog circuit is a **type of electronic circuit that processes continuous electrical signals, where voltage or current can vary smoothly over time rather than switching between discrete values**.

- Works with continuous signals (not just 0 and 1)
- Uses components like [[Resistor]], [[Capacitor]], and [[Inductor]]
- Common in [[Signal Processing]] and audio systems
- Sensitive to changes in voltage and current levels
- Found in amplification, filtering, and sensing systems
- Different from [[Digital Circuit]] which uses binary signals

An analog circuit processes continuously varying electrical signals to represent real-world information.

**Examples:**
- Audio amplifier in speakers
- Microphone signal processing
- Radio receivers
- Temperature sensor circuits
- Analog clocks and measurement devices
