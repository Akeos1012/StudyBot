**VRM** – (Voltage Regulator Module) is a component on the **[[Motherboard]]** that supplies and regulates the correct voltage to the **[[CPU]]** and other sensitive components like the **[[GPU]]**.

A VRM is a power control system that ensures the CPU receives stable and safe voltage.

- Converts higher voltage from the **[[PSU]]** into lower usable voltage
- Delivers stable power to the CPU and GPU
- Adjusts voltage dynamically based on workload
- Prevents damage from power spikes or instability
#### How it works?

PSU → VRM → CPU / GPU

The VRM takes raw power and “cleans it up” into precise, controlled voltage levels required by modern processors.

#### Why it is Important?

- Ensures system stability
- Prevents overheating and hardware damage
- Supports high-performance computing and overclocking
- Directly affects CPU performance reliability

In short, A VRM is a motherboard component that regulates and stabilizes power delivery to the CPU and GPU to ensure safe and efficient operation.

VRM = regulator that adjusts and stabilizes power
