### First Known Analog Computer

**Antikythera mechanism** - An ancient Greek <u>analog computing</u> device designed to <u>predict astronomical positions and eclipses decades in advance</u>. Discovered in a shipwreck off the island of Antikythera in 1901, it dates to around 150–100 BCE and is often regarded as the world’s first known mechanical computer.

- **Date of origin:** ca. 150–100 BCE
- **Discovery site:** Antikythera shipwreck, Aegean Sea
- **Material:** Bronze gears in a wooden case
- **Function:** Predict solar/lunar eclipses, planetary motions, Olympiad cycles
- **Current location:** National Archaeological Museum
