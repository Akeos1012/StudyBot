**Control Bus** – A control bus is a **set of electrical pathways in a computer system that carries control signals between the CPU, memory, and other hardware components to coordinate and manage operations**. It does not carry data itself, but instead carries instructions that control how data is transferred and processed.

- Sends control signals such as read, write, interrupt, and clock signals
- Coordinates communication between the [[CPU]], Memory, and input/output devices
- Works alongside [[Data Bus]] (data transfer) and [[Address Bus]] (location selection)
- Managed by the [[System Bus]] architecture
- Ensures proper timing and synchronization of operations
- Critical for execution control in computer systems

The control bus is a key part of the system bus that manages and coordinates how different hardware components communicate and operate together.
