**Transistor** - A **tiny electronic switch or amplifier** used in computers to control the flow of electricity, representing binary states (**ON = 1, OFF = 0**).

It is one of the most fundamental building blocks of modern computing systems and is used to create more complex components like **[[Logic Gates]]** and ultimately the **[[CPU]]**.

- Acts as an electronic switch (ON/OFF)
- Amplifies electrical signals
- Forms the building blocks of digital circuits
- Enables binary computation inside computers

#### Why it is Important?

- Enable binary computation (0 and 1)
- Allow miniaturization of computers
- Increase processing speed
- Reduce power consumption compared to older technologies like [[Vacuum Tubes]]

A **[[Transistor]]** does not exist alone in computing systems. It is part of a layered ecosystem that builds from **materials → electrical behavior → circuits → processors → system performance**.
