### The First Concept of a Programmable General-Purpose Computer

**Analytical Engine** - It was a mechanical computing machine designed by **Charles Babbage** in the 1830s and is often considered the first concept of a modern computer. While developing the simpler **Difference Engine**, Babbage realized a <u>machine could do more than repeat one calculation</u>, so he designed a <u>general-purpose machine</u> that could <u>follow different instructions</u> using **Punch Cards**.

![[Pasted image 20260616142051.png|408]]

The Analytical Engine introduced ideas still used in computers today:

- **[[Mill]]** → processing unit similar to a [[CPU]]
- **Store** → memory similar to [[RAM]]
- **Punch Cards** → input and instructions
- **[[Printer]]** → output
- **[[Conditional branching]]** → decision-making logic

Although it was never fully completed, the Analytical Engine became the foundation for the idea of programmable computers.

More than 100 years later, these concepts appeared in **[[ENIAC]]**, one of the first programmable electronic general-purpose computers.
