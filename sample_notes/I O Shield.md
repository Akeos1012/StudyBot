**I/O Shield** – An I/O shield is a thin metal plate attached to the back of a computer case that covers and protects the **[[Motherboard]]** input/output ports.

An I/O shield is a metal panel that protects and aligns the motherboard’s rear ports.

- Covers the back openings of the computer case
- Protects motherboard ports from dust and damage
- Helps align ports properly with external devices
- Reduces electromagnetic interference (EMI)

#### Why it is important?

- Prevents dust and debris from entering the system
- Protects ports from physical damage
- Ensures proper alignment when plugging cables
- Improves overall system durability

In short, an I/O shield is a metal plate that protects and aligns the motherboard’s rear input/output ports while preventing dust and damage.
